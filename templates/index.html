Exploratory Analysis Byte 2

Rachel Wong

Question: Has the number of Mexican citizens crossing the border increased or decreased?

Exploration: I explored this question by creating a bar graph for each of the three U.S. borders that the observed immigrants were attempting to cross. The x-axis shows the year the numbers were collected and the y-axis displays the number of US immigrants counted. The bars represent the total number of immigrants apprehended by the border patrols, with the light blue part representing how many of them came from Mexico.

Findings:

Immigration numbers, in general, have greatly decreased since 2000 at every border. The number of The number of immigrants from Mexico have also decreased since 2000 at every border, as well. There are higher numbers of Mexican immigrants coming from the southwest border, which makes sense geographically.

Further analysis:

I would be interested to dive deeper and look into the specifics of each sector and state. It would also be cool to see a graphical mapping of the numbers from this dataset. I would also look into more background information about the border patrol and how they apprehend illegal immigrants. Also, I would see if there have been any policy or procedural changes that could explain the extreme drop in illegal immigration numbers.
