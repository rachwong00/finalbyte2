Quality: About This Data Set

Is the data complete?

The data is not complete. There was a row of data taken from the coastal border that I omitted as soon as I loaded the data because it only had data for the first five years. One concern I have is that this is only the count of people that have been accosted by the border patrol, so this is only a "partial measure of the flow of people entering the United States". The true illegal immigration numbers/distribution could be much different because it is impossible to locate and count every illegal immigrant in the United States.

Is the data coherent?

Given the current political events, I do think that the data makes sense. Looking at the data from multiple other sources and in the general news, there have been many different analysis reports concluding that the number of illegal immigrants, both in general and from Mexico, have decreased over the last 16 years.

Is the data correct?

The data does seem to be correct. I looked into the NA's found in the first row for the coastal border and found that the Livermore sector of the border patrol was shut down after 2004, which accounts for the missing data. Livermore is also more inland California, so I feel that excluding it from our analysis is acceptable.
